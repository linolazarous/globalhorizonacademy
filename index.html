Frequently Asked Questions

Find answers to common questions about Global Horizon Academy

Global Horizon Academy offers three key differentiators:

  1. Dual Diploma System: Students earn both their national diploma and an International Baccalaureate, ensuring global recognition while maintaining local relevance.
  2. Global Standardization: Our curriculum is consistent across all campuses, allowing seamless transitions between locations without academic disruption.
  3. Specialization Tracks: Beginning in Grade 10, students pursue focused pathways (STEM, Humanities, Creative Arts, or Sustainable Development) with university-level resources.

Our admissions process consists of four stages:

1. Online Application: Submit basic information and academic records through our portal (takes about 20 minutes).

2. Diagnostic Assessment: Complete our online competency evaluation in mathematics, language arts, and critical thinking (90 minutes).

3. Family Interview: Virtual meeting with admissions staff to discuss educational goals and expectations (45 minutes).

4. Placement Decision: Notification within 2 weeks of completing all steps, with detailed academic pathway recommendations.

We accept applications year-round with rolling admissions, but recommend applying at least 3 months before your desired start date.

We offer comprehensive English language support through our English as an Academic Language (EAL) program:

  • Intensive Prep Course: 4-week summer program focusing on academic English (optional)
  • Dedicated EAL Classes: Daily language development sessions tailored to proficiency level
  • Content Support: Subject teachers trained in sheltered instruction techniques
  • Peer Tutoring: Matching with native English speakers for conversational practice
  • Multilingual Staff: Counselors and administrators fluent in 12 major languages

Most students achieve full academic English proficiency within 6-12 months of enrollment.

Academic Continuity: Because all campuses follow the same curriculum framework, students can transition without falling behind or repeating material.

Simplified Process: Transfers require just 30 days notice and completion of a brief orientation module about the new location's specific resources.

Popular Options: Many families take advantage of our "Semester Abroad" program, spending one term at another campus to experience a different cultural environment.

Digital Nomad Program: For Grades 11-12, students can rotate between up to 3 campuses per year while maintaining consistent academic progress.

Our University Preparation Program begins in Grade 9 and includes:

Academic Preparation

  • Personalized course selection advising
  • Advanced research methodology training
  • Standardized test prep (SAT/ACT/IELTS/TOEFL)
  • University-level writing workshops

Application Support

  • Dedicated college counselor from Grade 10
  • Essay writing bootcamps
  • Interview preparation simulations
  • Application deadline tracking system

Our graduates have been accepted to top universities worldwide, including Harvard, Oxford, Tsinghua, ETH Zurich, and the University of Cape Town.
